# Documented performance venue research register

Review date: 16 July 2026

## Scope

Three independent research passes covered India, Europe and the Americas, and
Asia-Pacific, Africa, and the Middle East. A public marker required a source
which names both Chhau and a specific theatre, auditorium, amphitheatre, or
other identified performance venue. The accepted set records places found and
verified in this review. It does not claim every Chhau performance in history.

## Excluded leads

- Hawaii's 1978 Asian Dance Festival, Japan tours, and city-only touring lists.
  Chhau activity was reported, but no theatre was named.
- Les Orientales in Saint-Florent-le-Vieil and the 1984 Los Angeles Festival of
  Masks. The evidence identifies a festival or producing museum, not the exact
  performance stage.
- London, Cairo, Rome, Cologne, Marseille, Aix-en-Provence, Mondeville, Seoul,
  Incheon, Bogotá, and Caribbean diaspora leads. A city, organisation, teaching
  visit, or generic Indian-arts activity does not establish a venue performance.
- Harvard University, Kansas University, University of Waterloo, American
  College of Greece, IIT Guwahati, IISER Pune, and other campus-only leads. The
  available source did not name a theatre or auditorium.
- Singapore Esplanade. No official archive or Chhau-specific programme was
  found. The old atlas marker remains excluded.
- Metropolitan Museum of Art, New York. The documented appearance located in
  review concerned Odissi, not Chhau.
- Volos Theatre and the New York production of Antigone. The source did not
  establish either work as a Chhau performance.
- Future schedules and advance-only listings. They remain outside the completed
  performance layer until evidence confirms the event occurred.
- The Stein Auditorium, India Habitat Centre. The official archive schedules a
  Seraikella Chhau programme, but no completion record was found.

Nairobi remains a documentary milestone only. UNESCO records the 2010
inscription decision there, not a Chhau performance.
