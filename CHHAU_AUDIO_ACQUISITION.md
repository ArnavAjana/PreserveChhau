# Chhau audio research and acquisition plan

Research date: 14 July 2026

## Publication decision

No located recording passes both checks required for this project:

1. It documents the named Chhau tradition, ensemble, or instrument setting.
2. It has public-domain or CC0 status, or written permission broad enough for
   streaming and redistribution inside this downloadable project.

The exact CCRT recordings below are useful permission leads. They are not
included in `public/audio`. Download access and fair-dealing language do not
grant republication rights.

## Mayurbhanj

D. N. Patnaik documents dhol, chadchadi or kadka, dhumsa, and mahuri in one
Mayurbhanj ensemble account. He also describes an earlier Pharikhanda and
Paikali setting with mahuri, dhol, and dhumsa. These contexts should remain
separate.

Source article: <https://archive.org/details/dli.ministry.11087>

Strongest permission lead:

- Mayurbhanj Chhau Dance, CCRT-366-SP

- Ogg: <https://archive.org/download/dni.ncaa.CCRT-366-SP/CCRT-366-SP.ogg>
- Duration: 21 minutes 43 seconds

## Seraikella

Jiwan Pani lists dhol, tikra or nagara, chadihadi, dhumsa, and mahoori in a
direct comparison of Seraikella and Mayurbhanj accompaniment.

Source article: <https://archive.org/details/dli.ministry.11084>

Strongest permission leads:

- Saraikella Chhau of Odisha, Volume I, CCRT-954-SP:
  <https://archive.org/details/dni.ncaa.CCRT-954-SP>
- Volume I MP3:
  <https://archive.org/download/dni.ncaa.CCRT-954-SP/CCRT-954-SP.mp3>
- Saraikella Chhau of Odisha, Volume II, CCRT-955-SP:
  <https://archive.org/details/dni.ncaa.CCRT-955-SP>
- Volume II MP3:
  <https://archive.org/download/dni.ncaa.CCRT-955-SP/CCRT-955-SP.mp3>

The catalogue reproduces the historical title exactly. It should not be read
as a present-day geographic claim. Both records state `Rights: CCRT` and omit
performer names. They stay excluded.

The manuscript also records a conflicting secondary account involving veena,
mridangam, and pakhawaj in an earlier Seraikella setting. CC0 veena files exist,
but they document Carnatic music, not Seraikella Chhau. They are poor substitutes
and should not appear in the reader.

## Purulia

alongside Jhumur singing and spoken or sung introductions in documented

## Why generic CC0 files were rejected

The search found CC0 recordings of wedding dhol, Holi street music,
Maharashtra dhol-tasha, wedding shehnai, and Carnatic veena. None documents the
regional Chhau setting named by the book. Several also omit performer identity
or performance permission. A shared instrument family does not establish a
Chhau style, local construction, playing method, ensemble role, or repertoire.

## Permission request

Ask CCRT for a written grant covering selected short excerpts. Request:

- online streaming and repository distribution
- downloadable eBook use
- trimming and browser transcoding without pitch or tempo changes
- performer, musician, troupe, piece, place, date, and recordist credits
- confirmation of the instruments heard in each excerpt
- the required credit line and any limits

CCRT contact page: <https://ccrtindia.gov.in/contact/>

## Original recording brief

The cleanest production route is a short session with named musicians from
each tradition. Record isolated strokes or notes, one short phrase per
instrument, one ensemble cue, and one 20 to 30 second identified passage.

Store 48 kHz, 24-bit WAV masters. Produce small Opus or MP3 files for the web.
Keep a signed record covering the sound recording and recorded performance,
along with permission to host, download, trim, normalise, and transcode.

Required credit pattern:

`[Instrument or piece], performed by [names], [troupe], recorded at [place] on [date] by [recordist]. Used with permission from [rights holder].`

Until the permissions arrive, the reader should state that recordings have
been identified but reuse permission and performer credits remain pending.
